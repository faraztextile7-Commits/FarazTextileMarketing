Faraz Textile Markiting Sourcing Services

End-to-end sourcing, inspection and compliance for international buyers — from fabric verification to final shipment acceptance.

Quality Assurance Procedures

Pre-Production Management

  • Conduct pre-production meetings with vendors to align specs, timelines and responsibilities.
  • Finalize fabric & trim requirements, approved vendors and sampling schedule.
  • Review production planning, line allocation and capacity to avoid delays.

Fabric & Material Control

  • Track fabric status from order to delivery and verify quantities against POs.
  • Perform incoming fabric inspection and acceptance sampling.
  • Coordinate lab testing for fiber content, colorfastness, dimensional stability and performance.

Ongoing Production Oversight

  • Monitor production progress and critical control points.
  • Carry out inline audits and corrective action follow-ups.
  • Prepare and submit production reports and documentation to buyers.

Quality Audit — Pre‑Production Stage

Fabric Inspection Procedure

  • Visual inspection for defects, slubs, holes and weaving/knitting faults.
  • Shade banding and cross-dye checks to ensure color uniformity.
  • Check roll measurements, lot numbers and delivery documentation.

Fabric Performance Testing

  • Arrange laboratory testing: colorfastness, pilling, shrinkage, tensile strength, and GSM.
  • Provide test reports and a pass/fail recommendation based on buyer tolerance.

Inspection Preparation & Meetings

  • Prepare inspection plan, checklists and sampling protocol prior to production start.
  • Conduct pre-production meetings to confirm technical packs, measurement charts and QC acceptance criteria.

Final Inspection

Our Final Inspection team is staffed by qualified QA professionals who verify each lot against international standards and buyer specifications. Inspections are carried out in-house or via accredited labs depending on the testing required.

What We Inspect

  • Visual inspections for workmanship, stitching and finishing details.
  • Quantity, packing, labeling and carton marking verification.
  • Material evaluation: touch, hand-feel and odor checks.
  • Color & shade consistency across batches.
  • Measurement checks against size charts and tolerance levels.
  • Weight / GSM verification and fabric consumption checks.
  • Any additional buyer-specific tests or acceptance criteria.

Inspection Deliverables

  • Detailed inspection report with photos and defect classification.
  • Pass/fail recommendation and suggested corrective actions.
  • Packing list verification and shipment readiness confirmation.

Factory Evaluation & Compliance Audit

Factory Capability

  • Good factory setup and organized workflow.
  • Condition and suitability of machines and equipment.
  • Technical competency and demonstrated quality conformance.

Compliance & Labour Standards

  • Safe and healthy workplace environment.
  • No child labour or forced/involuntary labour practices.
  • Fair disciplinary practices and non-discrimination policies.

Working Conditions & Wages

  • Working hours and overtime checks against local law and buyer policy.
  • Fair wages, payroll records and statutory benefits verification.
  • Grievance mechanisms and worker welfare initiatives.

Sourcing — Strategic & Practical

Proper sourcing is the key to successful imports from Pakistan. We match each purchase order to vendors who can deliver on quality, cost and timeline while meeting technical constraints.

How We Select Vendors

  • Assess financial strength and ability to fulfill orders at scale.
  • Verify technical abilities including machinery, processes and skilled personnel.
  • Evaluate factory layout, workflow efficiency and sample room capabilities.

Sourcing Prerequisites

  • Financial stability and timely payment history.
  • Technical competency for product-specific requirements.
  • Demonstrable productivity, lead-time reliability and capacity planning.
  • Established sample room and pro-active QA procedures.
  • Vendor reputation supported by long-term buyer relationships.

When We Decline

  • We may refuse projects that fall outside local vendor capabilities or where compliance risks are high.
  • We prioritize long-term supplier stability over one-off low-margin orders that jeopardize quality.
